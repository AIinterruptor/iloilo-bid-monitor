Postings How to Bid
General guidance, not legal advice. This page summarizes publicly available requirements under Republic Act No. 12009 (the New Government Procurement Act) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), based only on provisions we could directly verify against the official IRR text. It is not exhaustive — project-specific requirements, current peso thresholds, and document checklists vary by procuring entity and project. Always confirm exact requirements with the relevant BAC office before preparing a bid.

1The Bidding Process

Under RA 12009's Implementing Rules (IRR Section 27.1), Competitive Bidding follows these stages:

  1. Publication / AdvertisementThe procuring entity posts the invitation to bid on PhilGEPS and its own website (this is what this dashboard tracks).
  2. Pre-bid ConferenceHeld at least 12 calendar days before the bid submission deadline. Prospective bidders can raise clarifications here.
  3. Eligibility ScreeningThe BAC checks submitted legal, technical, and financial documents against requirements.
  4. Receipt and Opening of BidsBids are submitted by the deadline and opened publicly.
  5. Evaluation of BidsThe BAC evaluates responsive bids. RA 12009 allows evaluation on Lowest Calculated Responsive Bid (LCRB) or, as a new option introduced by this law, Most Economically Advantageous Responsive Bid (MEARB) — the procuring entity specifies which basis applies per project.
  6. Post-QualificationThe lowest/highest-ranked bidder's documents are verified in full before award.
  7. Award of ContractThe contract is awarded to the bidder who passes post-qualification.

2PhilGEPS Registration

PhilGEPS registration is mandatory — IRR Section 20.3.1 requires all procuring entities to conduct procurement through PhilGEPS, and bidders must hold a PhilGEPS Certificate of Registration (Platinum Membership) to participate. Membership is valid for one year and must be kept current annually.

Documents required for Platinum Membership (IRR Section 20.2.9.1):

SEC / DTI / CDA RegistrationSEC for corporations/partnerships, DTI for sole proprietorships, CDA for cooperatives.
General Information Sheet (GIS)Updated, reflecting beneficial ownership information, filed with SEC. Required since July 2025.
Mayor's / Business PermitCurrent and valid.
BIR Tax ClearanceFinally reviewed and approved by BIR.
PCAB License and RegistrationFor contractors bidding on infrastructure/construction projects.
Audited Financial StatementsStamped "Received" by BIR or an accredited institution.

Foreign bidders: documents must be in English, or accompanied by a certified translation, and authenticated via a Philippine foreign service post or apostille (IRR Section 20.2.9.2).

3Small Value Procurement

Not every purchase goes through full competitive bidding. RA 12009 made Small Value Procurement (SVP) its own standalone mode:

ModeThresholdRequirement
Small Value ProcurementUp to ₱2,000,000At least 3 price quotations; if the amount does not exceed ₱2,000,000, one quotation is sufficient to proceed with evaluation.
Competitive BiddingAbove ₱2,000,000Full process per Section 1 above.

Some LGUs and barangays apply lower, income-class-tiered SVP thresholds for their own procurement. We could not confirm exact figures against the IRR — check with the specific BAC office for project-level thresholds.
